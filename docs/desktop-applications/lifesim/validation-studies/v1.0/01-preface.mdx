# Preface

LifeSim is an agent-based simulation model developed by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (USACE) to estimate potential life loss and direct economic damages 
due to natural and dam and levee failure events. 

An important part of supporting that development is validating the results of LifeSim against historic events. Life loss estimates from LifeSim are a 
component of the risk assessment process used by USACE in its Dam and Levee Safety programs to estimate infrastructure risk and make decisions on managing risk. 
Validation studies provide a measure of confidence and an improved understanding of potential uncertainties in life loss modeling.

Recent efforts to develop dam or levee failure case histories have yielded tremendous advances and insight into consequence modeling. These insights have 
resulted in several improvements to the methodology built into LifeSim, including updated fatality rate relationships, a refined definition of hazard exposure, 
improved logic of vertical evacuation to include lack of mobility, improved vehicular evacuation logic, and improved stability criteria for structures, vehicles, 
and people exposed to flowing water.

## Validation Studies Available

A collection of the validation studies completed are provided for public access. All models have been updated in 2025 to LifeSim version 2.1.5.

levee failure posed valuable insights. <TableReference tableKey="validations-preface"/> below lists details on each study's respective failure, location, date, fatalities, and 
evacuation performed (if any).
