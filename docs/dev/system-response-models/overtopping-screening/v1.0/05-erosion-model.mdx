# Erosion Model

The erosion simulation proceeds in two sequential phases for each Monte Carlo realization at each hazard level. The first phase models the removal of
protective grass cover under sustained overtopping flow. The second phase models the progressive erosion of the exposed embankment soil. Both phases
are implemented in `OvertoppingModel.SimulateErosion()`.

## Phase 1: Grass Cover Removal

When grass cover is present (`GrassCoverQuality` is not `NoGrassCover`), the model tracks the cumulative excess discharge volume until the grass cover
fails. The grass cover removal algorithm is based on CIRIA research into tolerable time-varying overflow on grass-covered
slopes.<Citation citationKey="Hughes2015" /><Citation citationKey="Thornton2014" />

### Critical Discharge

The critical discharge ($q_c$) is the unit discharge at which grass damage initiates. It is computed from the critical velocity ($u_c$), the friction
factor ($f$), and the slope angle:

<Equation equationKey="overtopping-screening-eq-critical-discharge" equation="q_c = \frac{f}{2 g \sin\theta} \cdot u_c^3" />

where $g$ is gravitational acceleration (9.81 m/s²), $\theta = \arctan(S)$ is the slope angle, $S = 1 / \text{DownstreamSlope}$ is the slope
(V/H), and $u_c$ is the critical velocity from the grass cover classification. The critical discharge is computed in SI units internally; for US
Customary analysis, the result is converted to ft²/s by dividing by $(0.3048)^2$.

### Erosional Limit Volume

The erosional limit volume ($VET_c$) is the cumulative excess discharge volume per unit width that must be exceeded for complete grass cover removal:

<Equation equationKey="overtopping-screening-eq-vet" equation="VET_c = \frac{G_f \cdot f}{2 g \sin\theta}" />

where $G_f$ is the erosional limit from the grass cover classification (lb·s/ft³). For US Customary analysis, the result is converted to ft³/ft by
dividing by $(0.3048)^2$. For SI analysis, $G_f$ is first converted to SI units by multiplying by 0.3048 before the calculation.

### Cumulative Excess Volume

At each time step, the model computes the excess discharge above the critical threshold and accumulates the volume using the trapezoidal rule:

<Equation equationKey="overtopping-screening-eq-excess-volume" equation="V_{\text{cum}}(t) = V_{\text{cum}}(t - \Delta t) + \frac{\Delta t_s}{2} \left[ \max(q_t - q_c, 0) + \max(q_{t-1} - q_c, 0) \right]" />

where $V_{\text{cum}}(t)$ is the cumulative excess volume at time $t$, $\Delta t_s$ is the time step in seconds, $q_t$ is the discharge at the
current time step, and $q_c$ is the critical discharge.

Grass cover removal occurs when $V_{\text{cum}} > VET_c$. Once the grass cover is removed, the erosion phase begins immediately at the same time
step. If no grass cover is present, the model enters the erosion phase directly at the start of the simulation.

## Phase 2: Embankment Erosion

Once the grass cover is removed (or if no grass cover is present), the model computes the erosion rate at each time step using the excess shear stress
equation.<Citation citationKey="Hanson2011" /><Citation citationKey="WinDAM" />

### Flow Depth on Slope

The flow depth on the downstream slope is computed using Manning's equation for steady uniform flow:

<Equation equationKey="overtopping-screening-eq-flow-depth" equation="h_s = \left(\frac{q \cdot n}{k_n \sqrt{S}}\right)^{0.6}" />

where $h_s$ is the normal flow depth on the slope (ft or m), $q$ is the unit discharge at the current time step, $n$ is Manning's roughness
coefficient (sampled), $k_n$ is Manning's equation coefficient (1.486 for US Customary, 1.0 for SI), and $S = 1 / \text{DownstreamSlope}$ is the
slope (V/H).

### Applied Shear Stress

The shear stress applied to the embankment surface by the overtopping flow is:

<Equation equationKey="overtopping-screening-eq-shear-stress" equation="\tau = \gamma_w \cdot h_s \cdot S" />

where $\tau$ is the applied shear stress (psf or Pa), $\gamma_w$ is the unit weight of water (62.4 lb/ft³ for US Customary, 9810 N/m³ for SI), and
$h_s$ is the flow depth from <EquationReference equationKey="overtopping-screening-eq-flow-depth" />.

### Erosion Rate

The erosion rate is computed using the excess shear stress equation:

<Equation equationKey="overtopping-screening-eq-erosion-rate" equation="\varepsilon = K_d \cdot \max(\tau - \tau_c, \, 0)^\alpha" />

where $\varepsilon$ is the erosion rate (ft/hr or m/hr), $K_d$ is the erodibility coefficient (sampled from the PERT distribution), $\tau$ is the
applied shear stress from <EquationReference equationKey="overtopping-screening-eq-shear-stress" />, $\tau_c$ is the critical shear stress (sampled
from the PERT distribution), and $\alpha$ is the erosion exponent (`ErosionExponent`, default 1.0).

When `ShearStressMethod` is set to `Exclude`, the critical shear stress term is removed and the erosion rate becomes
$\varepsilon = K_d \cdot \tau^\alpha$.

### Cumulative Erosion and Breach

The cumulative erosion depth is accumulated at each time step:

<Equation equationKey="overtopping-screening-eq-cumulative-erosion" equation="D(t) = D(t - \Delta t) + \varepsilon(t) \cdot \Delta t" />

where $D(t)$ is the cumulative erosion depth at time $t$ and $\Delta t$ is the time step in hours (matching the units of $\varepsilon$).

Breach occurs when the cumulative erosion depth equals or exceeds the critical erosion depth:

<EquationNoRef equation="D(t) \geq D_c" />

Once breach is detected, the simulation for that realization and hazard level terminates immediately.

## Critical Erosion Depth

The critical erosion depth ($D_c$) represents the perpendicular distance that the downstream embankment face must erode inward to intersect the
upstream edge of the dam crest. The model assumes that erosion of the downstream face is uniform across the entire slope surface — the downstream face
translates horizontally into the embankment until it reaches the upstream crest control point.

<Equation equationKey="overtopping-screening-eq-dc" equation="D_c = \max\!\left(\frac{W_c}{\sqrt{1 + 1/S^2}}, \; D_{\min}\right)" />

where $W_c$ is the crest width (`CrestWidth`), $S = 1 / \text{DownstreamSlope}$ is the slope (V/H), and $D_{\min}$ is the minimum critical erosion
depth (2.0 ft for US Customary, 0.6 m for SI). This calculation is implemented in `OvertoppingModel.RunAsync()`.

The minimum critical erosion depth prevents unrealistically small breach thresholds for narrow or zero-width crests.
