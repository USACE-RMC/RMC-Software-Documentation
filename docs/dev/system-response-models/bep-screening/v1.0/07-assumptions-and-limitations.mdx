# Assumptions and Limitations

The BEP screening model applies several simplifying assumptions relative to the full toolbox models. These assumptions are deliberate design decisions for screening-level analysis &mdash; they reduce input requirements and generally produce conservative results to prevent false negatives. Each assumption is documented with its rationale, effect on results, and the conditions under which it may not hold.

## Key Limitation: Infinite Blanket Assumption

The infinite blanket assumption (assumption 1 in <TableReference tableKey="bep-screening-table-assumptions" />) is the most impactful screening simplification. When `UseFullHead = true`, the full net driving head ($H = \text{HW} - \text{TW}$) is applied directly at the downstream toe without any reduction for head loss along the seepage path. In reality, a finite blanket of length $L_3$ would reduce the excess head at the toe to:

$h_o = H \times \dfrac{L_3}{L_{\text{total}}}$

For a typical embankment with $L_3$ significantly smaller than $L_{\text{total}}$, this ratio can be well below 1.0, meaning the actual excess head at the toe is a fraction of the net driving head. The infinite blanket assumption eliminates this reduction entirely, resulting in uplift pressures that can be substantially higher than reality.

This assumption is applied in conditions 3 and 4 (confining layer present, no daylighting exit) and can produce high probabilities of initiation (blowout or heave) even at moderate headwater levels. Engineers should be aware that screening results under these conditions may be significantly conservative and that a follow-up analysis with the full BEP toolbox models &mdash; where the actual blanket geometry can be specified &mdash; may produce materially different results.

## Monte Carlo Reproducibility

The screening model uses a fixed random number generator seed (`RngSeed = 42`) to ensure that identical inputs always produce identical outputs. This design choice supports regression testing and reproducible screening results. The seed is shared between the BT Case 4 and Schmertmann sub-models, meaning both sub-models draw from the same deterministic random sequence for a given run.
